Some of the visual effects are outdated but the movie seems to hold up really well. funny looking bullet wounds still do the job.

I thought there were some clever visual story telling points. Example being after the replicant almost kills him and Rachel comes in clutch, he takes a sip of some alcohol and because the glass and drink are clear and the shot is backlit, you can see the blood flow into the drink after he takes a sip.

I got what I thought I would theme wise. "Empathy Propaganda".
I think the movie brings up some questions worth asking-
what does it mean to be human?
How should death affect the way we choose to live?
How much of a baddie was Rachel for 1982?
Why isn't ryan gosling in this film?

Some of the movie lost me. how he got from the snake to the first replicant didn't make too much sense to me. I couldn't hear everything perfectly though and the spanish captions were a bit distracting.

Movies like this fall in a funny place for me. I didn't find the original bladerunner fun or entertaining in the way that I find my favorite movies but I enjoyed watching it a lot. I loved the expereince of watching it because it showed me something new. It's someone's favorite movie out there and there's a reason people love it. I love trying things that other people love that I don't understand to try and see what they see. I'm glad i've learned to love movies like this.

This movie makes me really want to watch 2049 again.
